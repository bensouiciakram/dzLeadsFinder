# 2. Revenue Model Options and Recommendation

## Option A — Single Tier + Credit Packs (Recommended)

**V1 model:**
- Free tier: 15 credits/month, 30 searches, CSV export disabled OR watermarked
- Paid tier "Starter": 1,500 DZD/mo → 200 credits/mo (10 DZD/contact, matches DzLeads' ratio)
- Add-on credit packs: 500 DZD / 75 credits (≈ 6.7 DZD/contact — cheaper than base rate, incentivizes top-ups)

**Pros:**
- Fits Algerian buyer expectation (credit-based = pay-per-lead mental model)
- Matches DzLeads' structure (eases buyer comparison shopping)
- Add-on packs defend against the implied "Renew or buy more" dilemma buyers experience
- Uncapped MRR via add-on packs (heavy users self-upgrade)

**Cons:**
- Pricing ladder must be deliberately tiered in V1.5+ or you get sticker-shock on upgrade

**Mary's recommendation:** ✅ **Adopt Option A** for V1. It matches the verified market norm (credit-based), beats DzLeads on absolute price (1,500 vs 2,000 DZD/mo) while matching on per-lead economics (10 DZD/contact), and add-on packs create natural upsell without forcing tier upgrades.

## Option B — Three-Tier Subscription (V1.5 cadence)

- Free: 15 credits, no export
- Lite: 1,000 DZD/mo → 100 credits (10 DZD/contact)
- Pro: 2,500 DZD/mo → 300 credits (8.3 DZD/contact) — beats DzLeads' economics
- Plus: 6,000 DZD/mo → 800 credits (7.5 DZD/contact) + saved searches + priority support

**Pros:** Better ARPU segmentation; matches Linkiw's tiered approach.
**Cons:** Choice paralysis at V1; harder to communicate a wedge when you have three prices. ^Loss of clarity on the "I'm cheaper than DzLeads" positioning.
**Mary's view:** Reserve this for V1.5 when the buyer base is established. V1 needs one clear price point to anchor the wedge.

## Option C — Pay-as-you-go credits (no subscription)

- Free: 10 credits
- 100 DA / 12 credits (≈ 8.3 DZD/lead)
- 500 DA / 65 credits (≈ 7.7 DZD/lead)
- 1,500 DA / 200 credits (≈ 7.5 DZD/lead)

**Pros:** Lowest barrier, lowest commitment, fits Algerian distrust of auto-renew.
**Cons:** No predictable MRR (the user wanted recurring revenue, not transactional); harder to finance ops on volatile monthly volumes.
**Mary's view:** Consider offering as an *additional* purchase mode alongside Option A — let buyers buy either a subscription OR credit packs, picking what fits their cash-flow comfort. This hybrid is what DzLeads actually does ("All plans are one-time payments, no automatic renewal" + add-on credit packs).

## Final Revenue Model Recommendation

**Adopt Option A as V1 baseline, with Option C-style add-on packs available alongside the subscription.** Specifically:

| Tier | Price | Credits/mo | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free | 0 DZD | 15 | No card required; CSV export watermarked |
| Starter (sub) | 1,500 DZD/mo | 200 | ~10 DZD/contact, matches DzLeads ratio |
| Add-on pack | 500 DZD | 75 credits | One-time, no auto-renew, never expires |
| Add-on pack | 1,500 DZD | 250 credits | One-time, no auto-renew, never expires (bulk discount) |

**Recurring MRR target (uncapped — illustrative only, not a ceiling):**
- Months 1-2 (post-launch): 30-50 paid users = ~50-75K DZD MRR
- Months 3-6: 100-200 paid users = ~150-300K DZD MRR (assuming avg 1,500 DZD ARPU)
- Months 6-12: 300-500 paid users + V1.5 upsell to 5K DZD tier = ~500K-1M DZD MRR

**Note:** These are illustrative ranges derived from your 20K DZD ad budget + organic FB-group acquisition. The user explicitly removed the 45,000 DZD/month cap, so the model scales upward via V2/V3 upsell — not bounded by V1's starter tier.
