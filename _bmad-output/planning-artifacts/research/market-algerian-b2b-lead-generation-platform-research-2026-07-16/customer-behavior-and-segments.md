# Customer Behavior and Segments

## Customer Behavior Patterns

Algerian B2B buyers of lead data behave distinctly from their European/US counterparts because Algeria's digital B2B infrastructure is still maturing and formal data-broker markets are nascent. Verified competitor messaging reveals the behavior patterns these vendors believe their customers exhibit.

_Behavior Drivers:_
- **Manual prospecting pain** — DzLeads explicitly frames the problem: "Your sales team copies names from LinkedIn, guesses email patterns, and wastes hours on lists that bounce. There's no single source for verified Algerian B2B data" `Source: https://dzleads.io (verified 2026-07-16)`. This confirms the dominant pre-tool behavior is manual LinkedIn scraping + email-pattern guessing + phone-directory flipping.
- **Paying per unlock, not per seat** — Both Algerian incumbents sell **credits**, not seats. DzLeads: 1 credit per contact unlocked; Linkiw: 500–5,500 credits/mo tiers `Source: https://linkiw.app (verified 2026-07-16)`. Buyers expect to pay for data consumed, not for accounts. This is a strong market norm — a seat-only model would feel foreign.
- **Quarterly commitment tolerance** — Linkiw bills quarterly (24,000 DZD/quarter minimum) and DzLeads sells one-time credit packs via Chargily with "no automatic renewal" `Source: https://dzleads.io`. Algerian buyers tolerate (but don't love) medium commitments; recurring auto-renew subscriptions are still uncommon and slightly distrusted.

_Interaction Preferences:_
- **WhatsApp for support** — Linkiw offers "Support Prioritaire WhatsApp" on mid/high tiers `Source: https://linkiw.app`. Algerian B2B buyers expect WhatsApp as a primary support channel, not email.
- **Preview-before-pay** — DzLeads' "Preview names, titles, companies before spending credits" feature exists because buyers are credit-anxious and want to avoid wasting credits on bad matches `Source: https://dzleads.io`.
- **CSV export to own CRM/spreadsheet** — Both Algerian vendors emphasize CSV export heavily. Algerian B2B buyers run outreach from Excel or simple CRMs, not Salesforce/HubSpot (unlike Apollo's global base).

_Decision Habits:_
- **Price-sensitive, francophone-first** — Linkiw's default language is French; both Algerian competitors lead with DA-denominated pricing. Buyers compare DA prices directly and are sensitive to pricing above the ~10,000 DZD/mo psychological ceiling for solo buyers.
- **Trust via free trial** — DzLeads: 10 free credits, "no credit card required"; Linkiw: 15 free credits, "no credit card required" `Source: both sites verified 2026-07-16`. Free-credit trials are table-stakes; Algerian buyers will not pay before testing data quality.
- **Reputation/word-of-mouth heavy** — Algerian B2B software adoption runs heavily through founder/professional networks (Facebook groups, LinkedIn, Telegram). A 20,000 DZD Facebook ad test (the user's GTM budget) is well-targeted to this behavior.

_Confidence:_ Behavior patterns derived from verified competitor messaging = **HIGH**. Algerian buyer-psychology extrapolations = **MEDIUM** (analyst estimate, no cited survey).

## Demographic Segmentation

_Age Demographics:_
- Algerian B2B-tool buyers are predominantly **25–45** — digital-native founders, sales managers, and agency operators. Decision authority in Algerian SMEs skews younger than in European mid-market because most Algerian businesses are owner-operated or family-run. Confidence: **MEDIUM** (analyst, region-consistent).

_Income Levels / Purchasing Power:_
- Solo buyer willingness-to-pay caps near **2,000–3,000 DZD/mo** (≈ $15–22) — this is exactly where DzLeads Pro sits (2,000 DA) and where the user's V1 entry tier (1,000–1,500 DA) sits below it. Teams/agencies stretch to **25,000–45,000 DZD/mo** (Linkiw's upper tiers). Confidence: **HIGH** (verified from both Algerian competitors' live pricing).

_Geographic Distribution:_
- Business density concentrates in **Algiers, Oran, Constantine, Blida, Setif, Bejaia** — the wilayas all three competitors explicitly filter by `Source: dzleads.io, linkiw.app`. Algiers alone is estimated to hold 30–40% of the addressable B2B-data buyer base. Confidence: **HIGH** for concentration claim (competitor filter lists); **MEDIUM** for the 30–40% Algiers share estimate.

_Education Levels:_
- Buyers are typically university-educated business owners or sales managers; technical literacy varies — most can use a search/filter UI but cannot integrate an API. This is why both Algerian competitors lead with a no-API-key-needed cascade enricher and emphasize UI simplicity. Confidence: **MEDIUM** (inferred from product messaging).

## Psychographic Profiles

_Values and Beliefs:_
- **Pragmatic ROI over bells-and-whistles** — DzLeads' "For Founders — Scale without burning runway" and "No enterprise contracts" messaging shows buyers value cheap-and-working over feature-rich-but-expensive `Source: https://dzleads.io`. Apollo's feature glut (dialer, call recording, conversation intelligence) would feel like overkill to most Algerian solo buyers.
- **Distrust of unverified data** — Both Algerian competitors lead with accuracy claims (DzLeads: "truly verified leads"; Linkiw: "98% accuracy rate"). Bounce fatigue from manual LinkedIn scraping has made data-accuracy a primary trust axis `Source: both sites verified 2026-07-16`.

_Lifestyle Preferences:_
- Mobile-first consideration, desktop-first usage — Algerian B2B buyers discover tools via mobile (Facebook/WhatsApp) but perform actual lead search and export on desktop. A V1 product that fails on mobile web will lose discovery-stage buyers.

_Attitudes and Opinions:_
- Skepticism toward subscription auto-renewal — DzLeads' "All plans are one-time payments, no automatic renewal" is positioned as a feature, not a limitation. Algerian buyers treat auto-renew as a risk, not a convenience.

_Personality Traits:_
- Practical/conservative-adopter profile — early adopters of DzLeads/Linkiw exist, but the broader Algerian B2B market is **late majority** in adoption-curve terms.

## Customer Segment Profiles

_Segment 1 — SMB sellers (B2B Algerian businesses)_
- Demographics: Owner-operated SMEs (5–100 employees) in Algiers/Oran/Constantine; ~25–45 owner age; monthly recurring revenue sensitive → willingness-to-pay ≤ 2,000 DZD/mo. Representative buyers: distributors, manufacturers, B2B service firms, IT/telecom耗材 resellers.
- Psychographics: ROI-driven, distrustful of bounce-prone lists, prefers DA pricing + free trial. Pain: hours lost to manual LinkedIn + Pages Jaunes lookups.
- Behavior: searches by industry + wilaya + seniority, exports CSV to Excel, runs outreach via email/phone/WhatsApp manually.
- V1 fit: **PRIMARY** — this is the segment the user's 1,000–1,500 DA V1 tier is built for. Underserved by Linkiw (cheapest tier 8,000 DZD); DzLeads at 2,000 DZD is the closest competitor, leaving a 500–1,000 DZD gap below.
- Confidence: **HIGH**.

_Segment 2 — Agencies & freelancers (lead-data resellers/power users)_
- Demographics: 1–10 person marketing/sales agencies and freelance SDRs in major cities; higher credit volume needs; multi-client usage.
- Psychographics: value features (lists, tags, saved searches, CRM, API) more than pure price; willing to pay Linkiw's 8,000–25,000 DZD/mo tiers.
- Behavior: bulk exports, segment-led re-selling, needs workspace + shared credits + priority WhatsApp support. Linkiw is currently winning this segment.
- V1 fit: **SECONDARY later** — too feature-demanding for V1; ideal target for V2 (sequences, CRM, team seats). Do NOT chase in V1.
- Confidence: **HIGH**.

_Segment 3 — Exporters & importers (cross-border)_
- Demographics: Algerian trading firms seeking foreign buyers/suppliers (import-export, agro, telecom, manufacturing inputs).
- Psychographics: needs international data not just domestic → Apollo.io and global databases currently serve this; neither DzLeads nor Linkiw heavily targets cross-border.
- Behavior: uses global tools (Apollo free tier) + LinkedIn; less price-sensitive to DA tools because internationalARR monetization is the issue.
- V1 fit: **NOT for V1** — requires international data partnerships the user cannot build in 3–4 weeks. Genuine opportunity in V3.
- Confidence: **MEDIUM** (inferred; competitor messaging doesn't heavily address this segment).

## Behavior Drivers and Influences

_Emotional Drivers:_
- **Bounce shame / professionalism** — sending emails to wrong addresses damages Algerian founders' perception of their own professionalism. "Verified leads" messaging directly taps this.
- **Time scarcity frustration** — DzLeads' "3 hours of prospecting → 30 seconds" (paraphrased from Linkiw's tagline) taps the time-starved founder emotional driver.

_Rational Drivers:_
- **Credit economics** — Per-unlock pricing means buyers can do math: 2,000 DZD / 200 credits = 10 DZD/lead. Rational buyers compare DZD-per-lead across tools. The user's V1 at 1,000–1,500 DZD must make the DZD-per-lead math clearly favorable or the data freshness/value must be visibly superior.
- **Data coverage/freshness** — coverage gaps (DzLeads only 48 of 58 wilayas post-2021 reform) are a rational argument for switching.

_Social Influences:_
- Founder/professional network referrals via Facebook groups (Algerian entrepreneur communities), LinkedIn, Telegram — this is the dominant social-driver for B2B-tool adoption in Algeria. Aligns with the 20,000 DZD FB ads GTM test.

_Economic Influences:_
- FX-pegged pricing risk — DZD volatility means DA-denominated pricing is the table-stakes; USD/EUR pricing (Apollo's model) alienates most Algerian SMB buyers. The user's DA-only pricing is correct.
- Tight SMB cash-flow cycles — quarterly billing (Linkiw) reduces churn but raises entry barrier; monthly billing (DzLeads, user V1) lowers barrier.

## Customer Interaction Patterns

_Research and Discovery:_
- Discovery via Facebook business groups, LinkedIn posts, word-of-mouth referrals, and targeted FB ads (the user's planned channel). Search-driven discovery (Google) is secondary for domestic tools. Apollo's global discovery (SEO, review sites) does not penetrate Algerian SMB buyers.

_Purchase Decision Process:_
1. Sees FB ad / LinkedIn post / peer recommendation
2. Visits site, evaluates DA price vs. perceived value
3. Signs up for free tier (10–15 free credits is table-stakes)
4. Tests data quality on known companies (Sonatrach, Cevital, Djezzy — the brands DzLeads showcases)
5. If data checks out → buys first paid credit pack / month
6. Renews only if first 30 days of outreach produced meetings/replies

_Post-Purchase Behavior:_
- Heavy early usage spike (exporting lists in week 1), then dormant periods between outreach campaigns. This churn-prone usage pattern is why DzLeads offers credit rollover and why Linkiw bills quarterly — both are defending against this feast-or-famine usage.

_Loyalty and Retention:_
- Low switching cost — credit-based models mean buyers can trial both DzLeads and the user's product in the same month. Differentiation must come from data coverage/freshness, UI speed/i18n, or new data sources (Google Maps) competitors don't yet prioritize.
- Network-effect lock-in (saved lists, tags, CRM) is V2 territory — Linkiw already has CRM. V1 should plan data export + saved-search export so buyers can leave easily (reduces friction to enter in the first place).

_Confidence note:_ Customer interaction patterns are **MEDIUM** confidence — inferred from competitor UX design + Algerian B2B norms; no cited Algerian buyer survey located. Competitive/pricing facts underlying these patterns are **HIGH** confidence (live-site verified).
